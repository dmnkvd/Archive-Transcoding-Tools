THE RELATIVITY OF CLASSIFICATION

Welcome to a short story of an object's meaning...

Some believe the world is composed of essential elements. Perhaps all things derive from the circle. The basic shape of unity.

As time progressed, so has the complexity of our perceptions, of our meaning-making…

We soon began to give things names, to differentiate them from one another.

By naming things in relation to one another, we were able to draw comparisons between them.

Of course, the whole process is relative. Meaning-making, as most things in the natural world, does not happen in precisely delineated, ordered steps.

The further we layer the complexity of the meaning of our object, the bigger the variations will likely become. The more possible interpretations the descriptor can have, the more possible answers will appear.

No group exists that was once not a disparate array of individual items. A badge itself is a fascinating collection of signs assembled together…

The message encoded in the badge is historicised. It has changed over time, and will continue to do so. What might the words mean in a year, a decade, a century?

Perhaps we can rejoice in the fact that we are looking at this badge at the same time, and might ascribe a similar meaning to it, than with a century of time inbetween us. This meaning might change soon. But for now, we may relax and look at the badge.
